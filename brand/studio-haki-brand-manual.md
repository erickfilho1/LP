# Studio Haki Brand Manual

Version: 1.0 draft  
Created for: Eric Filho / Studio Haki  
Reference direction: minimalist black-and-white studio marks, spark symbols, strong wordmarks, Supafast-style premium SaaS structure

## Brand Essence

Studio Haki is not a freelance design service. It is a recurring creative studio plugged into the operation of agencies and infoproduct businesses.

Core idea:
- Plug a creative studio into your operation.

Brand promise:
- More predictable creative delivery for agencies and infoproduct teams that cannot let design, video, or landing pages block revenue.

Brand attitude:
- Rebel, premium, direct, operational, sharp.

Brand line candidates:
- Nao buscamos clientes. Buscamos parceiros de longo prazo.
- Plugue um estudio criativo na sua operacao.
- Menos freelancer solto. Mais previsibilidade criativa.
- Sua agencia vende. A Haki ajuda a entrega nao travar.

## Logo Concept

Name:
- Studio Haki

Primary verbal hierarchy:
- `HAKI` is the main word.
- `STUDIO` is the category/signature.

Symbol name:
- Haki Spark

Symbol meaning:
- Spark: creative ignition.
- Cross/connector: the studio plugged into the client's operation.
- Sharp red cut: a "hack", interruption, action mark, and urgency signal.
- Four directions: design, video, landing pages, and operational flow.

Why this route:
- It respects the user's references: clean black background, white geometric spark, compact premium mark, and strong studio wordmark.
- It avoids a generic "agency logo" and gives Studio Haki an ownable symbol that can work as favicon, stamp, watermark, social avatar, loader, card icon, and hero asset.

## Logo Color Rules

### On Black Background

Background:
- `#0D0D0D`

Logo:
- White wordmark
- White symbol
- Red cut accent

Use when:
- Landing page hero
- Premium portfolio sections
- Social covers
- Dark proposals

### On White / Light Background

Background:
- `#FAFAFA` or `#FFFFFF`

Logo:
- Black wordmark
- Black symbol container or black mono symbol
- Red accent allowed

Use when:
- Documents
- Contracts
- Light sections
- Minimal portfolio grids

### On Red Background

Background:
- `#FF0F33`

Logo:
- White wordmark
- White mark
- Black cut accent only when needed

Rule:
- If the background is red, the logo should be mostly white. Do not use red logo elements on red backgrounds unless separated by white or black.

### On Blue Background

Background:
- `#012871` or `#00153A`

Logo:
- White wordmark
- White symbol
- Red accent optional, but use sparingly

Rule:
- Blue is a trust/technical support color, not the main brand hero color.

### On Image / Busy Background

Use:
- White mono logo over dark overlay
- Black mono logo over light overlay

Do not:
- Place the logo directly over high-detail imagery.
- Add glow, shadow, bevel, outline, or gradient to force contrast.

## Clear Space

Minimum clear space:
- Use the height of the red cut accent as the minimum buffer around the logo.
- If applying in UI, use at least `32px` clear space around the horizontal lockup.

Do not:
- Put text too close to the mark.
- Place the logo inside a tight pill unless the mark has breathing room.
- Crop the spark.

## Minimum Size

Digital:
- Horizontal logo minimum width: `120px`
- Stacked logo minimum width: `96px`
- Symbol minimum size: `32px`

Favicon:
- Use symbol only.
- At `16px`, remove the red cut if it becomes noisy.

## Incorrect Usage

Do not:
- Stretch the logo.
- Rotate the wordmark.
- Use gradients inside the wordmark.
- Add drop shadows to the logo.
- Change the red to orange, purple, teal, or yellow.
- Combine red and blue inside the same CTA or logo treatment.
- Use the symbol repeatedly as a background pattern unless opacity is below `6%`.
- Place the red accent on a red background without contrast.
- Rebuild the wordmark using a random font.

## Color System

### Primary Colors

Black:
- Hex: `#0D0D0D`
- OKLCH: `oklch(15.91% 0.0000 89.9)`
- Use: main dark background, logo container, premium sections, footer, dark cards.

Red:
- Hex: `#FF0F33`
- OKLCH: `oklch(63.34% 0.2521 24.1)`
- Use: CTA, cuts, accent lines, active states, important tags.

White:
- Hex: `#FFFFFF`
- OKLCH: `oklch(100.00% 0.0000 89.9)`
- Use: text on dark, logo on dark/red/blue, high contrast surfaces.

Off-white:
- Hex: `#FAFAFA`
- OKLCH: `oklch(98.51% 0.0000 89.9)`
- Use: main light background, documents, soft page sections.

### Secondary Colors

Blue:
- Hex: `#012871`
- OKLCH: `oklch(30.78% 0.1329 261.3)`
- Use: trust, technical proof, secondary borders, selected badges.

Deep blue:
- Hex: `#00153A`
- OKLCH: `oklch(20.64% 0.0780 258.5)`
- Use: deep technical panels, not primary CTA.

### Neutral Scale

- `#FFFFFF` - pure white
- `#FAFAFA` - main light
- `#F5F5F5` - soft light section
- `#E0E0E0` - border
- `#9E9E9E` - muted text
- `#6E6E6E` - secondary text
- `#2E2E2E` - dark gray
- `#0D0D0D` - brand black

## Color Pairing Rules

Allowed:
- Black background + white text + red CTA
- White background + black text + red accent
- Red background + white logo/text
- Blue background + white logo/text
- Black card + red line + white typography

Avoid:
- Red text on blue background.
- Blue CTA with red icon.
- Purple, teal, orange, or neon green accents.
- Large red background with small black text.
- Low-contrast gray text on black.

CTA rule:
- Primary CTA should be red.
- Secondary CTA can be black, white, or outlined.
- Blue should not compete with red as the main action color.

## Typography

Primary display:
- Raleway

Primary body:
- Roboto Flex

Optional mono accent:
- Fragment Mono

Logo direction:
- The wordmark uses the attitude of Raleway Black: geometric, bold, compact, and slightly editorial.
- For production-grade brand finalization, the wordmark should eventually be outlined/customized in Figma or Illustrator so it is not dependent on live font rendering.

Type rules:
- Headlines: Raleway 800/900, tight tracking.
- Section titles: Raleway 700/800.
- Body: Roboto Flex 400/500.
- Eyebrows: Raleway 700 uppercase, letter spacing `0.08em` to `0.12em`.
- Buttons: Raleway 700 uppercase, letter spacing `0.06em`.

## Layout Personality

Studio Haki should feel:
- Minimal, but not sterile.
- Premium, but not luxury-fashion distant.
- Rebel, but not childish.
- Operational, but not corporate.

Use:
- Strong black and white contrast.
- Large negative space.
- Red bars/cuts as directional accents.
- Asymmetrical compositions.
- Cards with strict spacing and low decoration.
- Screenshots, Trello boards, creative examples, and proof rails as functional visuals.

Avoid:
- Rounded bubbly SaaS softness.
- Purple gradients.
- Over-centered pages.
- Overly playful illustrations.
- Generic agency blob graphics.
- Too many icons.

## UI Translation

### Navigation

Recommended:
- Floating compact nav inspired by Supafast.
- Black capsule on light or dark page.
- Logo left, links center, CTA right.

Logo usage:
- Use horizontal logo on desktop if space allows.
- Use symbol + `Haki` short lockup on mobile.

### Buttons

Primary:
- Red background `#FF0F33`
- White text
- Small radius: `6px` to `8px`
- Uppercase label
- Optional red cut/spark icon only if it does not clutter.

Secondary dark:
- Black background
- White text
- Thin gray border

Secondary light:
- White or transparent background
- Black text
- Gray border

Do not:
- Use overly rounded pill CTAs if the page is leaning more rebellious/editorial.
- Use both red and blue in the same button.

### Cards

Default card:
- White background on light page or `#151515` on dark page.
- Border: `#E0E0E0` on light, `rgba(255,255,255,0.12)` on dark.
- Radius: `8px` maximum for strict brand feel.
- Red accent line: `32px x 3px` or vertical cut.

Premium dark card:
- Background: `#0D0D0D`
- Text: white
- Accent: red
- Border: translucent white

### Brand Motifs

Use these motifs:
- Red cut line
- Spark connector
- Split before/after cards
- Plug-in diagrams
- Operational board visuals
- Numbered workflow blocks

Do not overuse the spark. One strong symbol beats twenty decorations.
